# 01 · Context

## The event

An **audio Space on X/Twitter**, hosted by **Horus** (emcee), bringing onto the stage representatives of three organizations collaborating around a single topic: **Semantic Delegation**.

The Space was held in **two parts**: the first was interrupted by a technical incident ("the first space had some type of issue and just shut down on us"), the second is the immediate resumption ("we are back on with part two"). A short gap of ~3 minutes separates the two.

- **MetaMask / Consensys**: the **Delegation Framework / Smart Accounts** team, invited to explain the framework and its intersection with Intuition.

## The central announcement

> **Five bounty missions are open** (announced "yesterday," so opened the day before the Space), with a total of **$7,500** in rewards. Applications at `[intuition].box/missions`.

These missions (numbered **9 to 13**) all sit at the intersection of **Intuition's knowledge graph** and **MetaMask's Delegation Framework**. See [04 · The 5 missions](04-missions.md).

## Why this topic is presented as "fundamental"

The host emphasizes that the topic is "absolutely fundamental to a lot of the next-generation capabilities coming to market." The real stakes:

1. **Programmable and safe permissions**: delegating to a dApp or an agent the right to act on your behalf, in a strictly bounded way.
2. **Semantics of trust**: Intuition's knowledge graph provides the "who/what is trustworthy" layer that purely literal delegations lack.
3. **Agentic future**: enabling AI agents to interact with large swaths of the ecosystem on your behalf, without the risk of overreach.
